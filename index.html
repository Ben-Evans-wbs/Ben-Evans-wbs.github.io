About Me

I am Benjamin Evans, an undergraduate reading Economics at Newcastle University and expecting a First-class degree. Born and raised in Liverpool, I have spent the past seven years translating curiosity into action—whether safeguarding swimmers as a qualified lifeguard, streamlining patient records in an NHS practice during the pandemic, or managing high-volume service at a busy airport restaurant.

These roles have cultivated two convictions: clear systems outperform ad-hoc effort, and composure is contagious. The same mindset led me to serve as Assistant Head of School and Head of the Economics Society, where I organised speaker events and investment simulations for more than 300 students, learning to balance fiscal responsibility with creative outreach.

My academic interests lie at the intersection of data analysis and public-policy impact; my personal interests lie on the Brazilian jiu-jitsu mat and the occasional ultra-marathon route—both reminders that endurance and adaptability are skills measured in years, not minutes. I am presently seeking internships that value rigorous quantitative insight as much as level-headed teamwork, and I welcome conversations that bridge the two.
